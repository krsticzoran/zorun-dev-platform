Three years ago, I was at the top of my amateur running career. In a relatively short period, I achieved results that I’m still proud of: **18:10 for 5K**, **38:20 for 10K**, and a **1:26:16 Half Marathon**. Those numbers weren't accidental; they were the result of hard work and a clear plan.

Then, a three-year break happened.

Life and responsibilities took over, and the fitness I built completely disappeared. But while the physical condition is gone, the experience is still there. I know exactly what it takes to reach a high-performance goal. At 45, I am starting over, but I know the way to the finish line.

### The Holy Grail: Why 2:59:59?

Three months ago, I started running again. It was then that the desire to run a marathon was born—a distance I have never officially covered before.

A goal like this demands time and discipline, resources that are often scarce for those of us in our mid-40s, balancing professional responsibilities and family life. In the psychology of a runner, a sub-3-hour marathon is the "Holy Grail." It is the ultimate benchmark of amateur performance, and that is precisely why it’s so compelling to me.

But for me, the goal isn't just about the numbers on the clock. It’s about the process of getting there—systematically and at a pace that respects my age of 45. It’s about building a sustainable path to excellence, one mile at a time.

### The Mission Beyond the Clock

Through this journey, my primary goal is to inspire people over 35 to embrace running. I want to share the immense benefits of this sport in our middle years, drawing from my own experience of how I felt—both physically and mentally—during the years I ran versus the years I didn't.

I will document the common mistakes runners in their mature years make: trying to train as if they were 20, or following programs designed for professional athletes. These shortcuts often lead to burnout or injury.

My mission is to prove that high-performance results, like a sub-3-hour marathon, are achievable at our age—if approached systematically and in sync with what our bodies actually require. It’s not about fighting time; it’s about working with it.

### The Roadmap and The Reality

This won't be a filtered success story. I will be sharing the full reality of this journey: the great runs, the setbacks, the injuries, and the struggle to stay disciplined on days when work and family life take center stage.

I’ll also be diving deep into the tools that help me stay on track—from performance shoes and GPS watches to the cameras I use to capture this journey. My goal is to find what actually adds value and efficiency to a runner’s life in their 40s.

The target is set for **December 2027**. It’s a long road, but the goal is to arrive there healthy, fast, and systematic. This is a documentation of that build.

**Welcome to Project 2:59:59.**
